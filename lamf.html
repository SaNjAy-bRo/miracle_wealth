FAQ

Questions Answered

Everything you need to know about pledging your mutual funds.

Do I have to pay interest on the entire credit limit?
No. Interest is calculated daily based only on the loan amount outstanding at the end of each day. If you borrow ₹50,000 out of a ₹5L limit and repay it in 10 days, you pay interest for those 10 days only. Zero usage means zero interest.
Will my SIPs continue after I pledge my funds?
Absolutely. Your existing SIPs continue without any disruption. New units purchased via SIP are not automatically pledged — only the units you specifically select for pledging are lien-marked.
Can I choose which mutual fund units to pledge?
Yes! You have complete control over which funds and how many units you want to lien-mark. Note: ELSS (locked-in) units cannot be pledged. Dividends from pledged funds still flow to you normally.
Does borrowing against MF affect my CIBIL score?
No credit enquiry is made that impacts your CIBIL score when checking eligibility. Approval is based on portfolio value, not just credit score — a major advantage over personal loans.
What happens if my MF portfolio value drops (margin call)?
If the market value falls and breaches the LTV ratio, you may be asked to either top-up with additional units, partially repay the balance, or add funds to bring the ratio back within limits.
