5 Biggest Blunders
Retirees Make

Most retirement plans quietly erode wealth — not through bad luck, but through avoidable mistakes. Do any of these sound familiar?

1

Relying Entirely on Fixed Deposits

FD returns of 6–7% barely beat inflation. After tax, your real return is often negative — your corpus is quietly shrinking every year.

2

Treating Life Insurance as an Investment

Traditional LIC-style plans return 4–7% with high lock-ins and surrender costs. Your money deserves better than a 30-year trap.

3

Randomly Investing in Mutual Funds

Owning 15+ funds with no strategy isn't diversification — it's chaos. Cluttered portfolios underperform and create zero alignment with your income goals.

4

Ignoring How Long Your Corpus Must Last

Retirement at 60 could mean 25–30 more years of expenses. Most plans don't account for longevity, leaving families financially exposed in their 80s.

5

Random Advise

Well-meaning relatives, local agents, or social media tips are not retirement strategies. Mis-sold products and uninformed decisions are the #1 source of wealth destruction in retirement.
